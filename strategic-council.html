Folio XIII  ·  Strategic Council  ·  Standing Body of Senior Counsel

The Strategic
Council.

A standing body of senior advisors — former heads of state, ambassadors, scholars, and civic leaders — who counsel the Foundation's Leadership Board on questions of substance and direction.

§ I   Mandate

What the Council does.

The Strategic Council convenes twice a year — once in Washington, once on the African continent — to advise the Foundation's Leadership Board on questions of substance, direction, and posture. Its members are appointed for staggered three-year terms by the Leadership Board, with the Founder serving as ex-officio convener.

The Council's deliberations are confidential. Its conclusions are made known to the Board and, where appropriate, to the public record under the Foundation's seal.

§ II   Inaugural Council

Members in formation.

The inaugural Strategic Council is being seated through 2026 and 2027. Members will be announced under the seal of the Foundation as appointments are confirmed.
