Standing
Partnerships.

The Foundation works with a small number of named institutional partners — universities, think tanks, corporates, professional associations, and civic bodies — whose mandate aligns with our own.

§ I   Categories of Partnership

Four kinds of standing relationship.

  1. Academic Partner

    Universities and research institutions on either continent who join the Foundation as standing scholarly partners — co-publishing briefs, hosting fellows, convening jointly under our seal.

  2. Policy Partner

    Think tanks and policy institutes whose research agenda aligns with the Foundation's. Joint working groups, co-authored publications, shared convenings.

  3. Corporate Partner

    Companies operating across the U.S.–Africa corridor that wish to underwrite specific lines of work — convenings, fellowships, sectoral dossiers — under transparent terms.

  4. Civic Partner

    Professional associations, diaspora organizations, and civic bodies who wish to mobilize their members around the Foundation's work and host its convenings in their own settings.

§ II   Terms of Engagement

How we work with partners.

  • Partnership PeriodThree years · Renewable
  • Joint OutputCo-published under both seals
  • Editorial IndependenceAlways · By charter
  • Naming RightsBy specific agreement
  • Public DisclosureAnnual report · Listed
  • Termination Clause90 days · Either party
§ III   Partnership Inquiry

Open the conversation.

Partnerships begin with a conversation, not a form. Tell us briefly who you are; we will write back to schedule the first call.
