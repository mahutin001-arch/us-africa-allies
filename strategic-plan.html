Folio V  ·  Strategic Plan  ·  MMXXVI – MMXXXI

A Five-Year
Founding Plan.

Six articles. Three operating horizons. One standing accountability. Adopted by the Leadership Board on the day of the Foundation's chartering, MMXXVI.

§ I   Preamble

What this plan is.

This document records the operating commitments the U.S.–Africa Allies Foundation has adopted for its first five years. It is intended to be read in public, audited in public, and amended in public. Each article below carries a name, a measure, and a date by which the Foundation will report progress to its membership.

We have written it deliberately short. The work we have set is ambitious. The plan should not be.

§ II   The Articles

Six commitments for five years.

I.
Convene with intent.
Diplomatic Convening · Year-Round

By 2031, the Foundation will have convened no fewer than thirty named gatherings — heads-of-state dialogues, ministerial roundtables, working groups — across the United States and the African continent, around questions of mutual consequence.

II.
Publish the record.
Policy & Research · Continuous

Every convening will produce a public, citable artifact: a brief, a joint statement, a working paper. By 2031, the Foundation will have built an open archive of one hundred such artifacts, freely accessible under our seal.

III.
Move capital both directions.
Economic Bridges · Quarterly Missions

Quarterly trade and investment missions in both directions, structured around named African markets and named American sectors. The Foundation does not broker deals; it convenes the conditions for them.

IV.
Organize the diaspora.
Diaspora Council · Standing

By 2028, the Diaspora Council will be a standing body of one thousand members across the United States, organized into chapters, with a published operating charter and a permanent seat at the Foundation's annual convening.

V.
Place the next generation.
Fellowships · Annual Cohort

Beginning in 2027, an annual cohort of fellows — half American, half African, all early-career — will spend twelve months in deliberate exchange under the Foundation's seal. By 2031, two hundred fellows will have completed the program.

VI.
Hold ourselves to the public ledger.
Accountability · Annual Report

The Foundation will publish a complete annual report on or before March of each year — programs run, dollars deployed, what worked, what did not. The first report will be issued in March 2027 and will set the standard for every report that follows.
